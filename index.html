What is Shawarma?

Shawarma is a popular Middle Eastern dish consisting of meat cut into thin slices, stacked in a cone-like shape, and roasted on a slowly-turning vertical rotisserie or spit. The meat can be lamb, chicken, turkey, beef, or mixed meats.

Originally from the Ottoman Empire (roughly modern-day Turkey), shawarma has become popular worldwide, especially as street food.

Key Ingredients

Meat

Chicken, lamb, beef, or mixed meats, marinated in spices

Vegetables

Tomatoes, cucumbers, onions, lettuce

Sauces

Tahini, garlic sauce, hummus

Bread

Pita bread or flatbread
